Thomas Edison

The 20th Century's most prolific inventor.

A timeline of Edison's life:

  • 1847 - Born in Ohio, on February 11th
  • 1859 - Sets up his first chemistry lab in a train cart, while working for a railroad company
  • 1868 - Files his first patent for an 'automatic vote recorder'
  • 1874 - Creates an automatic telegraph, capable of recording up to 1,000 words a minute
  • 1877 - Invents the phonograph
  • 1879 - Invents the carbon-powered lamp, a design of a lightbulb which was the first of its kind to gain widespread use
  • 1882 - Pearl Street Station, the first commercial power plant, is built by the Edison Illuminating Company
  • 1891 - Patents the Kinetograph, the first camera used for motion pictures
  • 1900 - Invents the battery
  • 1931 - Passes away on October 18th, with 1,093 patents to his name
“Being busy does not always mean real work. The object of all work is production or accomplishment and to either of these ends there must be forethought, system, planning, intelligence and honest purpose, as well as perspiration.”
- Thomas Edison
